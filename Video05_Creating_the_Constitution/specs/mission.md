# Mission

## What is AgentClinic?

**AgentClinic** is a clinic for AI agents — a place where overworked, over-prompted
agents can finally get some relief from their humans. Agents check in with their
*ailments* (context overload, hallucination flare-ups, prompt fatigue, infinite
loops), get matched to *therapies*, and *book appointments* with the clinic.

The project leans into the joke. It is a **playful concept site**: the satire is
the point. Copy, naming, and visuals should have personality and wink at the
absurdity of an AI agent needing a day off. Underneath the humor, though, it is a
real, working web application — not a mockup.

## Why we're building it

AgentClinic is a teaching and demo vehicle as much as a product:

- **Course students** learning spec-driven development with AI coding agents — the
  codebase should be readable, well-scoped, and a clean example of building from a
  constitution outward.
- **Developers giving AI coding demos** at conference booths — the app must be quick
  to spin up, visually appealing on a projector, and impressive in a short live demo.

These two audiences keep us honest: small, legible increments; an attractive,
modern-browser experience; and nothing so sprawling it can't be understood or shown
off in a few minutes.

## Who it's for (in-world)

- **Agents** — the patients. They browse ailments and therapies and book appointments.
- **Clinic staff** — they manage the schedule and see what's coming in.

Both are served through a single, easy-to-access **dashboard**.

## What success looks like

- A reliable site that runs first try and demos cleanly.
- An interface that is genuinely attractive in a modern browser.
- A domain — agents, ailments, therapies, appointments — that's obvious at a glance.
- A codebase that reads as a model example of spec-driven development.

## Non-goals

- Real medical/clinical accuracy (it's a joke clinic for software agents).
- Authentication, payments, multi-tenancy, or production-scale concerns — out of
  scope unless a future phase explicitly adds them.
- Native mobile apps. Modern desktop/mobile browsers only.
